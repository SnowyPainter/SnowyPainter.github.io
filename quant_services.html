Understanding Quant

We recognize the need for quantitative trading and introduce AI that works.

What are Quantitative Strategies?

Quantitative strategies leverage mathematical models and statistical methods to make trading decisions. These strategies are based on historical data, algorithms, and various financial metrics to generate signals for trading.

  • Data-driven: Decisions are based on data analysis.
  • Algorithmic: Strategies are often automated using algorithms.
  • Objective: Removes emotional biases from trading.

Advantages of Quantitative Strategies

Consistency

Quant strategies offer consistent results by relying on historical data and objective analysis.

Speed

Automated strategies can execute trades faster than any human trader, capturing opportunities in milliseconds.

Diversification

Allows for the diversification of portfolios by creating strategies across different markets and asset classes.

Financial AIs

Neo Strategy

It contains 3 models. Trend predictor, News reader, Price forecaster.

Most decisions are basically controlled by models.

Advantages:

  • High prediction accuracy, achieving at least 58% success rate, and up to nearly 90% in optimal conditions.
  • Improved returns, with maximum gains(in simulation) ranging from 19% to 25% and minimum losses improving from -9% to 0%.
  • Enhanced Sharpe ratio, indicating better risk-adjusted returns.
  • Stronger performance compared to MA breakthrough strategy.
  • Overall better performance, with prediction accuracy over 55%, making it superior to not using it.

Disadvantages:

  • Limited usefulness of price predictions alone; more effective when combined with trend forecasting.
  • May not perform well for certain stock pairs, leading to suboptimal results.

Compound Strategy

It works with only two mathematical models.

Advantages:

  • The CIR model provides a simulation of the initial asset price, which is crucial for generating trading signals based on the z-score between two assets.
  • This strategy is particularly effective in capturing optimal trading moments in volatile financial markets.
  • Pair trading allows for profit by adjusting the holding ratio between two stocks, potentially benefiting even when only one asset's value increases.
  • The CIR model's price simulation, combined with z-score calculations, can gather signals for all possible future scenarios, enhancing the speed of adjusting asset weights in pair trading.
  • Reduces the latency commonly associated with machine learning models and decreases trading costs by minimizing unnecessary signal adjustments.

Disadvantages:

  • Relies on accurate asset price simulations, which may not always reflect real market conditions.
  • Despite adjustments, achieving consistent accuracy above 90% for each asset using deep learning or time series models remains challenging.
